New Matchup Transition — 3 Approaches

How to signal "your vote is in, here's a fresh matchup"

A

Card Sweep Animation

After the 3rd vote, the voice cards and vote rows slide out (left/right or downward), a brief "Vote recorded" confirmation flashes in the center, then new cards slide in from the opposite direction. Total duration ~800ms.

Pros

  • Very clear visual break — old content physically leaves
  • The gap between exit and enter creates a natural "beat"
  • Confirms vote submission with the flash text

Cons

  • Slightly longer perceived wait (~800ms)
  • More complex to implement (exit + enter choreography)
B

Fade-Through with Confirmation Toast

After the 3rd vote, the entire main content area fades out (~200ms), a small toast/pill appears at the top saying "Vote recorded ✓ — New matchup" that auto-dismisses, then new content fades in (~300ms). Votes reset visually during the fade.

Pros

  • Simple, clean, and fast (~500ms total)
  • Toast directly addresses both confusions (vote saved + new matchup)
  • Easy to implement with CSS opacity transitions

Cons

  • Less dramatic — subtle users might still miss it
  • Toast needs careful positioning to not obstruct
C

Full-Screen Flash + Scale Reset

After the 3rd vote, a brief full-width color pulse (using the app's purple/cyan glow) washes over the content area. The voice cards simultaneously scale down slightly (0.95) then scale back up to 1.0 with the new data. A small "Matchup #N" counter increments visually.

Pros

  • Very noticeable — the glow pulse is unmissable
  • Feels like a "level up" moment, gamified
  • Matchup counter gives persistent context

Cons

  • Could feel flashy/distracting after many rounds
  • Full-screen effects can feel heavy on mobile

My Recommendation

Option B (Fade-Through + Toast) strikes the best balance. It's fast, clearly communicates both "vote saved" and "new matchup loaded," and is lightweight enough to not annoy after dozens of rounds. The toast directly solves both confusions A and B. It also fits the existing app's clean, minimal aesthetic.

Option A is great if you want more drama, Option C if you want gamification — but B is the sweet spot for clarity without fatigue.
